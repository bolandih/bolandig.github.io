Hamed Bolandi

I am a dual major Ph.D. candidate in Civil Engineering and Computer Science at Michigan State University under the supervision of Dr. Nizar Lajnef and Dr. Vishnu Boddeti . I work in the Human Analysis Lab (HAL) at MSU as a research assistant. My research is focused on integration of deep learning and finite element analysis. Specifically, I focus on prediction of stress distribution of structural components using convolutional neural network and attention transformers.

Research Interests: Machine Learning, Deep Learning, Finite Element Analysis, Structural Health Monitoring

Bridging Finite Element and Deep Learning: High-Resolution Stress Distribution Prediction in Structural Components

We proposed deep neural networks in the form of convolutional neural networks (CNN) to bypass the FEA. The model is able to predict high-resolution stress distributions on loaded steel plates with variable loading, geometries and boundary conditions.
